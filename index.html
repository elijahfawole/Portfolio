⭐ #1 · Highest Relevance — Brand Identity

Health Heralds Communications

Logo Design · Brand Identity · Colour System

The Challenge

Health Heralds Communications needed a brand identity that communicated credibility, clarity, and approachability in the health communications space. The logo had to work across all digital platforms, print media, and background variations without losing impact or legibility.

The Solution

I developed a logo system anchored on a stylised "H" lettermark that subtly incorporates a broadcasting/signal motif, communicating both health and communication simultaneously. The teal-to-navy colour palette balances trustworthiness with energy. I delivered three colour variations: full-colour, monochrome black, and reversed white, ensuring maximum versatility across any application.

The Process

The mark went through conceptual exploration focused on the dual meaning of "heralding", both a historical call to attention and a modern broadcast signal. The final icon fuses these in a compact, scalable form that reads clearly even at small sizes.

✅
Delivered a complete 3-variant logo system (full colour, monochrome black, reversed white), production-ready for digital, print, and merchandise applications. Directly comparable to the brand identity work Generation Science is commissioning.

⭐ #4 · Medium Relevance — Public Health Campaign

Project Eliminate Rabies

One Health & Development Initiative (OHDI) · Community Awareness

The Challenge

Communicate a complex government-NGO collaboration for a community-level dog vaccination campaign in Benue State, Nigeria. The design needed to carry three institutional logos while maintaining a clear public health message aimed at community audiences.

#7 · Creative Range — Film Banner

The Girl Who Cannot Love

Listening Birds Productions / AOP Productions / Big Way Entertainment · Film Promotion

The Challenge

Create a film promotion banner that captures the emotional weight of a drama about trauma and emotional barriers. The movie, which is a health advocacy project communicates the harmful impact of breast flattening, a harmful process aimed at making a girl child from being sexually attractive. The banner needed to work across social media, cinema, and press usage.

The Solution

A rich amber-over-dark cinematic tone palette anchors the emotional register. The title uses a dual-weight approach, thin serif tagline above the bold impactful main title. Character photography is cut out against the atmospheric background, creating depth. Three festival laurel wreaths are positioned without crowding, communicating prestige immediately.

Why It Matters for Generation Science

This piece demonstrates the ability to handle emotionally complex communication, a skill directly transferable to science storytelling that must connect with Millennial and Gen Z audiences, not just inform them. Good science communication, like good film, must first make an audience feel something, then it can make them think, and finally, move them to act.

🎬
Film selected for AFRIFF 2025, ENIFF 2025, and African SDGs Film Festival 2025, three major Nigerian and pan-African film festivals. The banner was used across all official festival submissions and social media promotion.
